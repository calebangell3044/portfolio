The roar of engines filled the stadium as Kira adjusted her helmet, the weight of corporate expectations pressing down harder than the g-forces she was about to endure.

"Thirty seconds to grid lock," the voice in her earpiece announced.

She'd trained for this moment her entire life. The Coronation Circuit—the race that would determine which corporation would control the eastern energy grid for the next decade. Millions of lives powered by whoever crossed the finish line first.

But as she looked at the other racers—their eyes hollow, their movements mechanical—she wondered if any of them remembered why they'd started racing in the first place.
